# Q1 Quarterly Review — April 26 → July 11, 2026

*First 90-day review. System formalized 2026-04-26; the review cadence had never run. Run two weeks early, off a month in which structure came apart.*

## 1. What Held, What Slipped

### Held — with zero erosion

- **Vaping.** Quit, stuck, no back-half decay. The single cleanest win of the quarter.
- **EMG / diagnosis.** Best-case result: active reinnervation confirmed.
- **Therapy entered.** Individual (Morin) producing real value; couples (Teresa) underway.
- **The system build.** Phase 0/1/2 shipped — substrate, MCP server, autonomous Todoist + calendar writes, weekly log as queryable library. More valuable so far as a *forcing function for organization* than as leverage. That's fine and expected.

### Slipped — all in the back half

- Exercise: real discipline gained, then back-half collapse.
- Sleep: awareness genuinely improved and held (Oura stayed on); the *floor* did not.
- Flexibility/mobility: yoga and stretching started to land, then fell off.
- Tirzepatide: 205 → 197 fast, then plateaued at the regulation ceiling — not a dose problem.
- Stimulant contract: broke three weeks running. A trend, not a slip.
- **The weekly check-in itself: a three-week gap.** The corrective mechanism went quiet.

## 2. Findings

### 2.1 The binary/continuous split

Sort the quarter by what held and what didn't and it isn't six separate slips — it's one signature, six times.

**What held was binary or one-and-done:** vaping (a decision that closed), the EMG, entering therapy, building the system. **What slipped was every continuous-recruitment practice:** lift cadence, sleep floor, yoga, the contract, the weight trajectory.

Things that required a daily track died when the track went. Things that were a closed decision or an identity shift held regardless.

### 2.2 The correction mechanism is coupled to the structure that fails

The track carries both *the doing* and *the noticing*. When it drops, both go at once — so the slide isn't seen while it's happening. This is not a capacity problem: May proves the capacity exists. It is a **coupling** problem, and it is the system's central structural vulnerability.

The check-in is self-cancelling under load: run when regulated, dropped when dysregulated — precisely when it is the thing that would catch the slide.

### 2.3 The old skeleton was arithmetically impossible

Placing the April skeleton into the real 14-slot week (weekday AM/PM ×5, plus fuller Sat AM/PM and Sun AM/PM) reveals **nine of fourteen slots carrying two or more commitments** — roughly 23 commitments in 14 slots, sequenced back-to-back, zero margin.

**It did not collapse under load. It was already at 100% utilization, and load revealed it.** May worked because nothing went wrong.

### 2.4 Surge, not baseline, is what buckles the system

The system is stable at baseline. It buckles on **surges**, and there are two named sources:

- **Expensive discretionary/recreational commitments** — volitional, scheduled in advance, visible before they land. *Filterable.* The "no" happens at the calendar, weeks ahead, from a regulated state — Ulysses-contract logic, decided cold.
- **Relationship emotional stress** — not schedulable, not filterable. Already the target of the highest-leverage work of the quarter (therapy). The system's answer to this surge source is the therapeutic track, not a filter.

June was both at once, with no reserve.

### 2.5 Slack cannot come from cutting Tier 1

Everything in Tier 1 is load-bearing; there is no fat to trim. Slack has to come from somewhere else:

- **Lowering the cost of what is already done** — see 2.7.
- **Recognizing the appetite for *more* as a load source, not spare energy.** The pull toward more is, under the system's own regulation protocol, an early-warning sign (stability → restlessness → the itch). It is not a legitimate competing priority. It is a signal.

### 2.6 The solo-regulation root

Therapist's theory, accepted as *something that has to be tried*: the real gap is the **absence of relational support**. Every maintenance and corrective loop is self-run. The compensatory behaviors fill the space where relational support would otherwise be.

This is consistent with everything the log shows. The check-in is self-run and self-cancels. The work triage is self-run and doesn't happen. Even this system is a more elaborate apparatus for regulating alone.

**Consequence: relational support moves out of Tier 3.** It is not a social nice-to-have — it is a candidate *regulation input*, on the order of sleep. Concrete handles: **Sam** and **Shalyn**.

> Surfaced in passing and worth keeping: Shalyn had been silently filtered out of the "counts as support" category — *"female, which for some reason I felt like didn't count."* An unendorsed rule that pre-disqualified a real source of support. If the theory in 2.6 is right, the bandwidth problem is partly a **counting** problem.

**The trade is expensive and unpriced.** It costs the scarcest currencies — capacity and vulnerability — and must be made from a depleted state. That is why it has never happened on its own, and why it needs structure rather than resolve.

### 2.7 Labor was bought. Coordination was not.

Chef, cleaners, childcare all remove **doing**. None of them remove **deciding, tracking, and chasing** — the coordination layer that fragments an executive-function-constrained week. That is the juice that's left, and it is a different substance from what has already been purchased.

Most of this quarter's rock list is coordination, not judgment: finding specialists, booking consults, chasing paperwork, transfer logistics. *The decisions require Matt. The phone calls do not.*

### 2.8 Motion over production (professional)

Work is fine on progress and wrong on shape: too many commitments, capacity spread thin, more motion than production. The fix — a prioritization and triage cut — has been deferred *for lack of time*, which is the thing the cut would create. **A closed loop, and the third instance of the same pattern** (check-in, work triage, relational investment: in each case, the mechanism that would create slack requires slack to run).

**The workday is the only place on the board with real slack in it**, hidden behind the fact that it is already occupied. Work triage is therefore not one rock among many — it is the **capacity generator** every other rock depends on.

### 2.9 The experiential pole

Named this quarter, previously absent from the architecture: **think less, feel/experience more.** Presence, body awareness, sacred sexuality, ego dissolution — one thread, not three.

This is not a seventh domain competing for tier space. **It is the counterweight to the entire cognitive apparatus.** The system is a thinking machine; the compensatory behaviors it fights (stimulants, phone, the itch) are all crude attempts to *stop thinking and drop into experience*. The system regulates away the broken exits from the head without providing a good one. An unmet need for a cognitive off-switch does not disappear — it finds the nearest exit.

Therefore: **protective, not additive.** It gets a **container, not a track**; the practice layer (breathwork, meditation, yoga) is structured, but the pole itself carries no deliverable. Critically, the practice layer is a *continuous-recruitment* practice — the exact category that collapsed — so it **requires a bad-week minimum** or it will be first to go, precisely when the machine is hottest and the exit most needed.

## 5. The New Skeleton (v2)

**Frame:** 14 slots — weekday AM/PM ×5, plus Sat AM/PM and Sun AM/PM as fuller half-days. Everything must live here; anything else gets fed into weekday work hours or doubled onto an evening parenting shift.

**Two alternating week shapes.** Both are complete weeks. Neither is a degraded version of the other.

### Week A — "Yoga week"

| | AM | PM |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | Addie shift | **LIFT 1** |
| **Tue** | **LIFT 2** | Addie shift |
| **Wed** | **LIFT 3** | Addie shift |
| **Thu** | Addie shift + **cardio 1** *(5am wake)* | **Emma** |
| **Fri** | **Emma + YOGA** *(WFH)* | Addie shift + **Lauren date** *(sitter)* |
| **Sat** | Tennis = **cardio 2** + Addie daycare | **Emma** *or* **GSD** |

### Week B — "Friend week"

| **Tue** | **LIFT 2** | Addie shift + **Emma (fold)** |
| **Wed** | Addie shift + **Emma (fold)** | **FRIEND** |

### How the swap works

Week B moves two parenting shifts — **Wed PM + Thu AM → Wed AM + Thu PM** — same count, rearranged. Emma sleeps over Tuesday and is in the family fold Tue PM / Wed AM. That yields:

- **Wed PM → FRIEND night** (the only source of a friend slot in the entire week)
- **Thu AM → LIFT 3** (replacing Week A's Wed AM lift)
- **Fri AM → cardio** instead of Emma + yoga

**Yoga is the trade.** Week A has yoga (Fri AM, WFH-enabled, with Emma); Week B has no yoga and stretching moves into the GSD block instead. One flexibility session lands every week either way.

Both weeks hold: **3 exclusive lifts** (rehab non-negotiable), **2 cardio**, **6 parenting shifts**, 1 flexibility session, 1 Lauren date, 4 Emma slots, 1 GSD block.

### Constraints that generated this shape

- **Lifts are exclusive** — nothing else in the slot. Three is a rehab non-negotiable.
- **No Emma sleepover can precede a 5am wake.** This killed a Wed-fold variant and is why the lift moves to Thu AM in Week B.
- **Yoga requires Friday's WFH morning.** It is not portable.
- **Lunchtime lifting is not viable** most weeks (occasionally Mon/Fri).
- **The Emma-fold move is gated on the relational work** — it requires Lauren's comfort and Emma's willingness, and is the *benefit-first integration* already committed to. The skeleton's only unlock and the integration strategy are the same action.

### The GSD block

**Four hours, one weekend afternoon. Existence is non-negotiable; the day is placed at the weekly check-in, from a regulated state, before the weekend fills.**

*If the check-in does not place it, it does not exist.* Placement is now a **mandatory check-in stage**, not an afterthought.

The block is deliberately *not* fixed to a day — weekend shape genuinely varies, childcare is scheduled weekly regardless, and a rigid slot would be brittle. The commitment is to the block's **existence and its advance placement**, not to a particular day.

Contents: the weekly check-in, stretching (Week B), and rock work. **Guard against raiding:** four hours minus check-in minus stretching is not four hours of rock work.

### The evening routine (outside the 14)

Reading · skincare · plan-the-day · teeth · stretching. **This is the only place in the entire week where the restorative slot and the experiential pole actually live.** Every other slot is allocated. It is not an optional addition — it is the container for two things this review named load-bearing, and it exists outside the 14 precisely so it can hold them.

### Known gaps in the skeleton — stated, not solved

- **Family-of-five time has no home.** Saturday post-tennis brunch is the nearest candidate (a texture change rather than a 15th slot).
- **Lauren has one exclusive slot (Fri PM); Emma has four.** Stated as architecture, not judgment — but romantic re-engagement with Lauren is live, and the structure currently allocates her the residual: the tired hour after bedtime on doubled parenting evenings.
- **There is still no genuine flex.** The GSD block is the only true slack, and it is already over-subscribed. **This is what the work triage is meant to solve** — the day is where the time has to come from.
- **Travel weeks (~every 3rd, Tue–Thu) void this pattern ~33% of the quarter.** No variant has been designed.
