# Initial Program Design

**Review trigger:** EMG results (~3–4 weeks out) or significant change in pec recruitment, weight trajectory, or tirzepatide tolerance.
**Phase:** Calibration (Weeks 1–2) → Build (Weeks 3+)

## Design principles

- **Strength-style format, holistic outcomes.** Lifts are programmed for visible progression. Cardio is programmed for VO2 development. Pec rehab is its own thread woven throughout.
- **Pec rehab philosophy: engaged rehab, not protective rehab.** Until EMG defines the actual denervation ratio, the working assumption is that every fiber the nerves can reach should be pushed to recruit. Denervated fibers won't fire regardless of effort; inhibited fibers respond to demand. Under-pushing leaves inhibited fibers under-recruited. Push hard, with isolation work as the primary driver and structural safeguards (pre-fatigue, contraction quality cues) ensuring compensators don't mask the pec.
- **Pec rehab runs 4–5x/week with real working sets,** not floor-level activation.
- **Lower body is currently undertrained relative to upper.** Tue is a dedicated lower day to rebuild that.
- **Upper body splits into pull (ahead) and push (engaged-rehab + compound).** Pull progresses normally; push uses isolation-first ordering and pec-engagement as the load-progression gate.
- **Cardio gets two real touches per week** (Mon finisher + Thu) plus tennis as bonus.
- **Every session ≤ 60 min active time.** No bloat.
- **Calibration first.** Weeks 1–2 establish baselines, especially on untested lifts (squat, deadlift, OHP, pull-ups). Don't chase max load yet — but on pec rehab, push intensity from day 1.

## Session templates

### Mon PM — Conditioning + Pec Rehab

**Goal:** Lower-demand session that hits cardio + a serious pec rehab block on the day energy is lowest.
**Time budget:** ~60 min.

1. **Warmup (5 min):** general movement, light cardio, dynamic mobility.
2. **Pec rehab block — engaged (20 min):** real working sets, isolation-focused, contraction quality monitored throughout.
   - High-to-low cable crossover (left arm) — 4×10–12, RPE 8 on top sets, pause and squeeze at bottom
   - Pec deck or machine fly — 3×10, slow eccentric, hold peak contraction 2 sec
   - Single-arm DB squeeze press (left, on bench) — 3×10
   - Single-arm wall press (left) — 2×8 controlled, used as an engagement check at the end
3. **Zone 2 cardio (20 min):** elliptical, bike, or treadmill incline walk. Target ~70% max HR.
4. **Optional core finisher (5 min):** plank variations, dead bugs, or bird-dogs (also serves spinal stabilization goal).
5. **Cooldown / mobility (5 min).**

> **Cueing throughout the pec block:** if the working set isn't producing a clear pec contraction, drop weight and re-establish — but do not drop effort. The right weight is whatever lets you push hard and feel the pec. If those two are in conflict, weight is the variable that moves.

### Tue AM — Lower Body

**Goal:** Rebuild lower body strength and capacity. Currently the most undertrained area.
**Time budget:** ~60 min.

1. **Warmup (8 min):** bike or row 3 min, then hip mobility (90/90, hip airplanes), ankle dorsiflexion, glute activation.
2. **Primary lift (15 min):** rotate weekly between:
   - Trap bar deadlift — 3×5, building load week-over-week
   - Back squat (cautious) — 3×6, conservative loading, form-first
   - Safety bar squat or hack squat as alternates if back squat form is off that day
3. **Secondary lift (12 min):** Romanian deadlift OR Bulgarian split squat — 3×8 per side
4. **Accessory (10 min):** leg press 2×12, walking lunges 2×10/side, OR machine hamstring curl 3×10
5. **Calf + core (5 min):** standing calf raise 2×15, hanging leg raise or cable crunch 2×12
6. **Cooldown (3 min).**

> **Calibration weeks:** First time on each primary lift, work up to a comfortable 5RM-ish set (RPE 7–8). Do not chase max. Establish a number we can build from.

### Wed AM — Upper Pull + Pec Rehab

**Goal:** Progress pulling strength (the area that's responding well). Maintain pec rehab frequency.
**Time budget:** ~60 min.

1. **Warmup (5 min):** band pull-aparts, scap pulls, light row.
2. **Primary pull (12 min):** rotate weekly:
   - Pull-ups (or assisted/eccentric variants) — 4 sets, AMRAP up to 8, build over weeks
   - Barbell row — 3×6
3. **Secondary pull (10 min):** chest-supported DB row OR seated cable row — 3×8
4. **Lat pulldown (8 min):** wide grip — 3×10. (Currently 160×10 comfortably; build from there.)
5. **Pec rehab block — engaged (12 min):** real working sets, not deload.
   - High-to-low cable crossover (left arm) — 3×10–12, RPE 8
   - Floor fly with DB — 3×10, slow eccentric, working weight
6. **Rear delt + bicep finisher (8 min):** face pulls 3×12, DB curl 3×10
7. **Cooldown (2 min).**

### Thu — Cardio (Home)

**Goal:** Real cardio progression. This is the day to suck wind on VO2 weeks.
**Time budget:** 30–45 min.

Alternating weekly:

- **Week A — Zone 2 long (40 min):** Peloton bike or tread incline walk at conversational pace. Target HR ~70% max. Build duration over weeks before raising intensity.
- **Week B — VO2 intervals (25–30 min total):**
  - 5 min warmup
  - **4×4 protocol:** 4 min at ~90–95% max HR (uncomfortable, can speak only short phrases) / 3 min easy recovery × 4 rounds = 28 min
  - 3 min cooldown
  - Modality: treadmill incline, bike, or rower. Pick what you'll actually do hard.

> **Calibration note:** First VO2 session, run 3×3 instead of 4×4 to establish tolerance. Build to 4×4 by week 3 or 4.

### Fri Late AM — Upper Push (engaged-rehab) + Pec Rehab

**Goal:** Push pressing forward with pec engagement as the gating criterion. Isolation-first ordering ensures the pec is genuinely fatigued before the compound.
**Time budget:** ~60 min.

1. **Warmup (8 min):** band work, scap activation, light DB press warmups, left pec pre-activation flexes.
2. **Pec rehab block — pre-fatigue, engaged (15 min):** pushed hard. The pec should be smoked before bench.
   - High-to-low cable crossover (left arm) — 3×10–12, RPE 8, pause at bottom
   - Pec deck or machine fly — 3×10, slow eccentric, peak hold
   - Single-arm wall press (left) — 2×8, engagement check
3. **Primary press (15 min):**
   - DB bench press — 3×8, RPE 7–8. Currently 60s × 10/10/8 to failure unfatigued; with pec pre-fatigued expect this to feel meaningfully harder. Start at the weight where pec engagement is perceptible throughout the set.
   - **Progression rule:** weight goes up when 3×8 is achieved with the pec engaged on every rep. If pec engagement fades mid-set, hold the weight or drop it. Pec engagement gates load progression — not the other way around.
4. **Secondary press (10 min):** rotate weekly:
   - Seated DB shoulder press — 3×8
   - Landmine press — 3×8/side
5. **Triceps + accessory (8 min):** cable triceps pushdown 3×12, lateral raise 3×12
6. **Cooldown (4 min).**

> **Critical philosophy:** Push hard. The pec's actual ceiling is unknown until EMG, so the goal is to find what fires today by demanding it. Isolation work before compound ensures bench is a test of pec recruitment under load, not a substitute for pec recruitment work.

### Sat — Tennis (or makeup gym session)

Default: Recreational doubles, 2 hours. Counts as movement, not as a programmed session. If a weekday session was missed: swap tennis for the missed session at the gym instead.

### Sun — Mobility

**Time budget:** 20–30 min.

- Overhead mobility: wall slides, dead hangs (if tolerated), thoracic extension on foam roller, banded pec stretch (gentle on left).
- Hip hinge mobility: 90/90, hip airplanes, deep squat hold.
- Ankle / calf mobility.
- Cervical mobility: gentle, no end-range extension.

## Travel week template (Tue–Thu away, hotel gym: DBs to 50 + cable)

When travel hits Tue–Thu, the week compresses:

| Day | Session |
|---|---|
| Mon | Normal — Conditioning + Pec Rehab |
| Tue (travel) | DB Lower Body — goblet squat 3×10, DB RDL 3×8, DB lunge 3×8/side, calf raise 2×15 |
| Wed (travel) | DB Upper Pull — DB row 3×8/side, single-arm cable row 3×10, pulldown if available 3×10, DB curl, face pull |

| Sat–Sun | Normal |

**Pec rehab on travel days:** Daily voluntary flexing continues. Add 1×10 single-arm wall press at hotel — costs nothing.

## Progression rules

1. **Pulling lifts:** Standard double progression. When top set hits the high end of the rep range with RPE ≤8, add load next session.
2. **Lower lifts:** Progress weight cautiously, RPE 7–8 ceiling for now while rebuilding.
3. **Pressing:** Pec engagement gates load progression. Push isolation hard from day 1. On compound press, progress when working sets are completed with perceptible pec engagement throughout.
4. **Cardio:** Zone 2 progresses by duration before intensity. VO2 intervals progress from 3×3 → 4×3 → 4×4 → 4×4 at higher intensity.
5. **Pec isolation:** Real working sets at RPE 8. Load progresses when reps and engagement quality both hold. Tempo and pause work remain non-negotiable structural elements.
6. **Tirzepatide weeks:** No load progression in the week of a dose escalation. Hold or reduce slightly.

## What gets tracked each session

(Detailed format defined in `log_format.md`.)

- Date, session type, duration
- Each lift: weight × reps × sets, RPE on top set
- Pec engagement quality on rehab work (1–5 subjective)
- Cardio: modality, duration, avg HR if available, RPE
- Notes: how the body felt, any symptoms, anything the trainer should remember next time

## Open questions for future revision

- After 4 weeks of tirzepatide: assess whether energy/recovery has stabilized; potentially raise training volume.
- Once squat/deadlift/OHP baselines are established: set 6-month strength targets.
- Add grip strength testing in week 2 or 3 for longevity baseline.
